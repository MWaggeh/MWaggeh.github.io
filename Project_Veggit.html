VEGGIT

"Reinvent yourself"
The Challenge: Wellness Tools
Context

This challenge consisted in designing for NWI, a fictional company that wanted to reinvent itself by developing tools to help people achieve their health and wellness goals. Even though NWI had many years of experience in the wellness field, their program had been slow to catch up with technology. They had seen a substantial drop in memberships and wanted to find a way to add value to their members. So the task to be completed was to:

  1. Create a digital wellness tool for their clients
  2. Update their image - create a new visual system that reflects their innovative and refreshed approach to wellness.
My Role

My role included all the design thinking process: from brainstorming for ideas to understanding the user, defining a final product, prototyping and testing it. This project was to be completed in 4 months, from June to September.

The UX Process

1. RESEARCH

With a first idea in mind, an App especifically designed to help vegetarians achieve their fitness goals via a nutrition tracker, I did and initial background research to understand more about the target group's perceptions. The methodologies used where surveys and also 5 interviews. I believed interviews would help getting a more complete picture of the target.

Although the survey and interviews results showed the majority of vegetarians do like taking care of their health & fitness, the interviews uncovered a major concern regarding my idea: tracking the nutrients you consume may be good but it can turn into an obsessive behaviour, to a point that you stop enjoying the simple fact of being vegetarian.

“I used to track some food choices but after some time it just takes up to much time to estimate portion sizes and add all items to the app”

“Good data, but time consuming to fill out”

“I feel like with an app I would give too much time to control my diet and it could turn into a little obsession”

Discovery

By putting these research notes into an affinity diagram I managed to identify potential pain points and opportunities.

User

Based on the surveys, I realized it was mainly vegetarians aged 24-33 that care more about their health but do not have that time to fully focus on it

PROBLEM DEFINITION

This insights helped me identifying what their real problem was: vegetarians that workout or exercise do not have enough time to plan their meals.

And motivated me to focus in another direction by addressing this problem with the following idea:

Final Solution

“An app for vegetarians to plan their meals based on the groceries they have purchased or that they have on their fridge”. No hassle, no overthinking, just delicious meals based on what they already have.
